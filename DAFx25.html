This webpage provides supplementary materials for the article titled "An Adaptive Wavelet-Based Algorithm for Assessing the Quality of the Attack Transients in Non-Percussive Instruments Sounds", submitted to the International Conference on Digital Audio Effects (DAFx) 2025. These audio samples are provided to enhance understanding of the methods and results discussed in the paper.

Accuracy and Robustness Analysis

The following plots present the histogram distributions of attack durations estimated using three different methods:

  • Weakest-effort method: A simple approach that estimates attack duration with minimal constraints.
  • -3 dB below the maximum value: Defines the attack duration threshold at -3 dB relative to the peak amplitude.
  • Pitch-synchronous wavelet transform (PSWT) method: A more refined technique that achieves more reliable estimates by separating the harmonic components from the noisy transient.
These methods were applied to 100 synthesized sounds with attack transients corrupted by enveloped white Gaussian random noise across six levels of signal-to-noise ratio (SNR): 120, 20, 12, 7, 3, and 1. Each histogram displays the distribution of attack durations, along with the average value and standard deviation for each method, highlighting their accuracy and robustness.

The figure below shows an example of the envelopes of a synthesized sound and the added noise. In this example, the Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) is -1 dB, meaning that the noise envelope reaches 1 dB more than the sound envelope. The attack duration \( t_{\text{off}} \) of the synthesized sound is also shown.

Below each histogram plot, HTML tables provide detailed data on the obtained results. These tables include:

  • The type of signal analyzed (sinusoidal, sawtooth, triangular, square, or trumpet-like).
  • The detected center pitch \( f_0 \).
  • The pitch standard deviation.
  • The frequency modulation \( f_m \) and the modulation depth \( c_{mp} \) of the signal.
  • The estimated offset time \( t_{off} \) of the attack transient.
  • The Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) level.
  • The average attack time estimation \( \mu \), along with the corresponding standard deviation \( \sigma \) and range for each attack duration estimation method.
  • The attack duration estimates for each synthesized sound across the three methods.
  • An audio player to listen to a sample of the synthesized sounds analyzed.
